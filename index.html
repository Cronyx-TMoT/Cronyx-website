Introduction

Cronyx was born from a simple question :

How to represent unexpected events in a calendar?

Imagine you have to leave for a trip to a distant place.

Traditional calendars show the final destination: the day of departure, the arrival time, the scheduled appointments.

The reality, however, is different.

Waking up, having breakfast, packing your luggage, taking transportation, dealing with traffic: these are all events that make up the same journey.

What if one of these events should take more time than expected?

A delay can affect everything that comes after. The arrival time changes, appointments shift, and the initial planning no longer represents the real situation.

Cronyx is born precisely to address this issue.

Its goal is not only to show when an event occurs, but also to represent the influence that an event has on another.

Because time is not just a simple line composed of isolated events.

It is an area full of causes and effects.

And Cronyx seeks to represent it in the most natural, dynamic, and human way possible.
